Explore the Coast

Sapelo Island

A state-protected island (barrier island) located in McIntosh County, Georgia, Sapelo Island is only accessible by airplane or boat, with the primary ferry coming from the Sapelo Island Visitors Center.

Explore Sapelo Island

St. Catherine's Island

St. Catherines Island is ten miles long and anywhere from one to three miles wide. .

Explore St. Catherine's Island

Cumberland Island

One of the largest undeveloped barrier islands in along the Atlantic coast, The island has one of the largest wilderness areas on the east coast.

Explore Cumberland Island

Ossabaw Island

Contains 9,000 acres of wooded uplands with freshwater ponds and 16,000 acres of marshlands interlaced with tidal creeks.

Explore Ossabaw Island

Wassaw Island

10,053 acres of marsh, mudflats, and tidal creeks, including approximately 7 miles of undeveloped beaches.

Explore Wassabaw Island

Wolf Island

Established in 1930 as a migratory bird sanctuary, consists of a long narrow strip of oceanfront beach backed by a broad band of salt marsh.

Explore Wolf Island

Tybee Island

Easternmost point in the state of Georgia, entire island is a part of the Savannah Metropolitan Statistical Area.

Explore Tybee Island

Sea Island

The surrounding marshland, through which visitors are able to drive, was immortalized in 'The Marshes of Glynn' by Sidney Lanier in 1878.

Explore Sea Island
